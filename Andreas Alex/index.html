Rafflesia Arnoldii

Rafflesia arnoldii produces the largest individual flower in the world. Some of its blooms can reach up to 3 feet (about 1 meter) in diameter and weigh as much as 24 pounds (around 11 kilograms). This extraordinary size is a striking contrast to its host plant, a vine-like plant in the genus Tetrastigma.

Unpleasant Odor: When the Rafflesia arnoldii blooms, it emits a foul odor often likened to that of a rotting corpse. This odor attracts flies and other insects, which serve as pollinators.
